No Man's Land Insignificance

No Man's Land

The land between trenches in WWI was known as “No Man’s Land” since no man could own it. However, this area of land was one of the least impactful things in WWI. This is shown by the 56,000 casualties and of those, about 17,000 were fatal. When the Central Powers’ mobilized over 25 million soldiers, over 400 times more people than those that were injured in No Man’s Land, No Man’s Land is an insignificant part of WWI. Along with that, the land was influenced by military strategies. Without those, No Man’s Land is even less influential than it was before, which is already very very low. Considering these factors, No Man’s Land didn’t affect WWI that much at all.
